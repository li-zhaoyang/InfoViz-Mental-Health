Countries in different income groups are troubled by different types of mental illness, so as people in different phase of their life. The charts below demonstrates these differences in multiple facets.

Considering types of mental illness in different income country groups, the most remarkable difference comes from conduct disorder and idopathic intellectual. Low-income countries suffer more of these two illness than higher income countries.

Ageness is also closely related to types of mental illness in people. Depressive disorder, anxiety disorders and other mental disorders grows as age grows. Nevertheless, Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder, conduct disorder and intellectual disability are more rarely seen in higher age groups.
